LAMINA

Materials science claims — about alloy microstructures, phase diagrams, diffusion barriers — are hard to fact-check from raw LLM output. Hallucination risk is high and numeric values matter: 'fcc + tau phase' is different from 'fcc + sigma phase,' and 'Ea ≈ 0.3 eV' is different from '0.5 eV.' A model that sounds confident and gets the number wrong is worse than useless; it's a research hazard.

Prep Questions

Q: Why LangGraph on top of pydantic-ai instead of a single unified agent?

A: Phase isolation plus durable checkpointing. A LangGraph state machine gives me explicit routing, resumable turns, and hard per-phase budgets — things that are hard to enforce when one LLM call silently decides everything.

Q: How does durable streaming survive disconnects?

A: The server runs the turn on a procrastinate worker that's independent of the client connection, and the client reattaches by event cursor on reconnect.

Q: How do you adapt pydantic-ai events to Vercel AI SDK v6?

A: A per-phase PhaseStreamEmitter wraps pydantic-ai's VercelAIEventStream, strips its turn-level Start/Finish/Done markers, and lets the turn runner emit those exactly once around the whole multi-phase turn.
